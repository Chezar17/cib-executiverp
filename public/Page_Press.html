PR-2026-004 Official Statement Public Advisory April 20, 2026
Commander Diraj Lakota - "KITA HARUS LEBIH KERAS KE GANG YANG KERJAANNYA JUAL SENJATA MULU!"

The Central Investigation Bureau today reaffirmed its commitment to dismantling organized criminal networks involved in the illegal trafficking and distribution of firearms across the region.

Commander Diraj Lakota, Director of the Central Investigation Bureau, issued a strong statement regarding the growing threat posed by armed street gangs and weapon suppliers:

“We must be tougher on criminal gangs whose only business is selling weapons. These organizations fuel violence, intimidate communities, and profit from fear. The CIB will not allow them to operate freely. We are expanding investigations, increasing intelligence-led enforcement, and working with our law enforcement partners to identify, disrupt, and prosecute every individual involved in the illegal weapons trade.”

Under Commander Lakota leadership, the Bureau has prioritized gang suppression operations, long-term intelligence gathering, and coordinated enforcement actions targeting those responsible for supplying firearms to violent offenders.

The CIB emphasized that illegal weapons trafficking remains a direct threat to public safety, often linked to robberies, assaults, extortion, and gang-related shootings. The Bureau stated that future operations will focus not only on gang members, but also on financiers, smugglers, brokers, and anyone assisting criminal enterprises.

Commander Lakota concluded by assuring the public that the Bureau remains fully committed to protecting communities and restoring order through decisive action, professional investigations, and relentless enforcement.

Issued by: Office of the CIB Director RELEASED: 20 APR 2026 · 06:00 HRS
