Restricted Access — Internal Pathway Only

The Bureau Does Not Recruit.

The Central Investigation Bureau selects. There are no open applications, no submission portals, and no waiting lists. If you have to ask how to get in — you are not ready.

Bureau Standard

This Is Not an Application Process

The Central Investigation Bureau operates differently from every other unit within the San Andreas law enforcement structure. We do not post vacancies. We do not run intake cycles. We do not advertise.

The selection of Bureau personnel is a matter handled internally — through observation, reputation, and direct approach. Those who have demonstrated the qualities we value already know who we are, and know how to find us.

If you are reading this page hoping to find a form or a process, understand that the path into the CIB begins long before any formal conversation takes place. It begins on the street, in your record, and in the way you carry yourself when no one is watching.

We are not looking for officers who want to try something new. We are not a stepping stone, a title, or a change of scenery. The Bureau demands a different kind of commitment — one that most officers are not prepared to make.

Those who belong here already feel it. They operate with a level of discipline and judgment that stands apart from their peers. They are not waiting to be noticed — they simply are.

There are no shortcuts and no exceptions. The Bureau's integrity depends on that standard being absolute.

What Matters to Us

The Standard

We are not evaluating credentials. We are evaluating character, conduct, and capability. These are the qualities that distinguish those who belong in the Bureau from those who do not.

Observation & Instinct

You notice what others miss. Your investigative instinct is not taught — it is demonstrated, consistently, in the field. You do not wait to be told what to look for.

Discretion Under Pressure

Information is handled with absolute care. Your judgment does not deteriorate when stakes are high. You understand what to say, what not to say, and why the difference matters.

Record Without Stain

Your conduct on file must be unambiguous. Excessive force, procedural violations, or a pattern of poor judgment are not starting points for conversation — they are the end of one.

Operational Maturity

You have earned the respect of your peers, not by rank alone but by how you work. You function within a structure while operating independently. You do not need to be managed.

Awareness of the Bureau

You have taken the time to understand what the CIB is, what it does, and what it stands for. You know who our people are. This is not a unit you stumble into.

Commitment, Not Curiosity

We have no interest in officers who want to "see what it's like." The Bureau requires officers who know why they belong here and are prepared to give what that demands.

The Pathway

How Entry Into the Bureau Works

I.
Make Yourself Known Through Your Work

The Bureau is watching long before you are. Officers who are considered for entry have already demonstrated something exceptional — in their arrest record, their conduct under scrutiny, their handling of complex situations, or the way they carry their department's name. There is no shortcut to this. Do the work.

II.
Approach a CIB Detective Directly

When you are ready — and you will know when you are ready — the path forward is a direct, professional conversation with a serving CIB Detective. Not an email. Not a form. A conversation. Introduce yourself. Be clear about who you are and why you are standing in front of them. If that conversation warrants continuation, it will.

III.
Prove Your Worth — Continuously

Entry is not a finish line. It is the beginning of a longer evaluation. Officers who join the Bureau do so knowing that they are expected to operate at a standard that justifies their place here, every day. Those who cannot maintain that standard do not stay.
