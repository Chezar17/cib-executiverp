§ II
Purpose & Justification

Why This Portal Exists

"Transparency is not a concession — it is a cornerstone. A bureau without a public face is a bureau without public trust. The CIB web portal is that face."

— CIB Command · April 2026
[ PUBLIC TRANSPARENCY ]
Civilian Accountability

The portal gives San Andreas citizens direct, real-time visibility into CIB's mandate, case activity, and wanted individuals — enabling informed cooperation and building institutional trust between the bureau and the public it serves.

[ OPERATIONAL INTEGRITY ]
Official Public Record

By maintaining a formal, live digital presence, CIB establishes a verifiable institutional record. Every division, every warrant, every leadership appointment is documented — accessible to citizens, press, and council alike.

[ THREAT COMMUNICATION ]
Active Warrant Registry

The Most Wanted registry functions as a public safety bulletin. Citizens can identify and report known dangerous individuals with active warrants — directly accelerating CIB's ability to close cases and protect San Andreas.

[ INSTITUTIONAL GROWTH ]
Recruitment & Force Expansion

A credible, well-designed public portal attracts qualified candidates. The recruitment section enables CIB to reach skilled civilian applicants and grow its detective force — a direct investment in long-term public safety capacity.

§ III
Council Case

Why the Council Should Fund This

01
State-Grade Public Safety Infrastructure

The CIB portal is not a private project — it is public safety infrastructure. The Most Wanted registry, the press room, and the threat level system directly serve civilian protection. State funding ensures this infrastructure remains live, maintained, and reliable.

02
Proven Operational Readiness

The portal is already built and operational. The council is not funding a concept — it is funding the maintenance and expansion of an active system with real data, real cases, and real users. ROI is immediate and measurable.

03
No Alternative Exists in San Andreas

No other law enforcement body in the state operates a comparable public digital presence. CIB's portal is a first — setting a standard for institutional transparency that the entire state benefits from. Funding this sets a precedent for accountable governance.

04
90% Conviction Rate Justifies Investment

CIB produces results. With a conviction rate of 90% and 50+ closed cases, the bureau has demonstrated operational excellence. Funding its digital infrastructure is funding a high-performing institution, not a speculative one.

05
Nexus Internal Portal — Future Capacity

Beyond the public portal, CIB has developed Nexus — a classified internal operations platform. State funding enables its full deployment, giving detectives secure, centralized access to case files, warrant status, and inter-division intelligence.

06
Press & Public Relations Capability

The portal's Press Room enables CIB to issue official statements, communicate with the media, and control the bureau's public narrative. This protects both the bureau and the Council from misinformation during active cases or public incidents.

§ V
Organizational Structure

CIB Divisions

C.I.D
Criminal Investigation Division
"The end justifies the means"

The backbone of CIB's prosecutorial power. CID agents handle San Andreas' most complex criminal cases — organized crime, financial fraud, homicide, and major felonies. Every case is built from ground up: from the first tip to the final courtroom verdict. CID does not act on assumption. It acts on proof.

Homicide & major crimes investigation
Financial crime & money laundering
Witness protection & informant management
Court liaison & evidence management
G.R.D
Gang Recon Division
"We see before they move"

Operating where conventional law enforcement cannot — deep within the neighborhoods, streets, and networks where organized gang activity thrives. GRD detectives are trained in surveillance and undercover operations, embedding themselves within communities to identify, monitor, and disrupt criminal networks before they escalate.

Gang threat assessment & territory mapping
Undercover infiltration operations
Inter-gang conflict early warning
Street-level real-time surveillance
§ VI
Investigative Process

How an Investigation Works

01
Phase One
Intelligence Intake

Tips, surveillance footage, informant reports, and inter-agency data are reviewed and assessed for credibility and urgency by CIB analysts.

02
Phase Two
Active Investigation

CID or GRD detectives are assigned. Evidence collection, suspect profiling, surveillance operations, and field work commence under strict protocol.

03
Phase Three
Case Development

All collected evidence is organized into a prosecutable case file. Chain of custody is maintained. Witnesses are secured and briefed by CID specialists.

04
Phase Four
Arrest & Prosecution

Arrest warrants are executed in coordination with field units. The completed case is handed to the DA with a full evidence package for prosecution.
