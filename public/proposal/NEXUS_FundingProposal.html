Section I · Overview
Platform Overview

What Is NEXUS?

NEXUS is a fully classified, role-authenticated internal intelligence portal built exclusively for CIB personnel — invisible to the public, inaccessible without verified credentials, and engineered from the ground up to handle the bureau's most sensitive operational data.

Access Control
Clearance-Gated Entry
NEXUS operates behind a role-based authentication gate. No detective enters without a verified badge and clearance tier — Detective, Senior, Command, or Council. Audit logging captures every session.
Architecture
Air-Gapped Intelligence
All intelligence data — informant registries, gang intel, weapons tracking, HVC profiles — exists entirely within NEXUS. No external APIs, no public-facing data paths. Zero exposure by design.
Scope
14 Operational Modules
From the dashboard intelligence overview to the GIU Weekly Report, NEXUS unifies 14 distinct operational functions into a single authenticated session — eliminating information silos across all CIB divisions and sub-units.

Section II · Modules
System Architecture

Fourteen Modules. One Platform.

Every CIB operational need is served through NEXUS's modular architecture. Each module is access-controlled, audit-logged, and designed to eliminate the paper-trail, spreadsheet, and off-portal communication gaps that expose sensitive intelligence.

Intelligence
Command Dashboard
The operational nerve centre. Every Detectives's first view on login — live session status, clearance level, portal health, and quick access to all modules. Senior Command can monitor active sessions in real time.
  • Real-time session and portal health monitoring
  • Clearance-personalised module availability
  • Live alert strip for threat status changes
  • Reduces time-to-access for critical data from minutes to seconds
Documentation
CIB Official Guidebook
The complete operational manual — SOPs, chain of command, rules of engagement, and division protocols — embedded directly inside NEXUS. No external links, no version drift, always current.
  • Instant access to procedures during live operations
  • Eliminates out-of-date printed or external copies
  • Single source of truth for conduct and protocol
Legal Reference
Penal Code Reference
The full San Andreas penal code embedded in-portal. Detectives can verify charges, cross-reference statutes, and confirm sentencing guidelines without leaving the secure environment mid-case.
  • In-portal legal reference during interrogations and write-ups
  • Reduces erroneous charging that creates case vulnerabilities
  • Keeps detectives in the secure environment at all times
Top Secret
Main Personnel Directory
The classified internal directory of all CIB personnel — rank, division, clearance tier, and contact chain. Access is restricted by clearance, ensuring sensitive command structures are never exposed to junior ranks.
  • Speeds coordination across divisions during multi-team operations
  • Clearance-gated: command hierarchy never exposed downward
  • Replaces insecure off-portal message chains for internal contact
Top Secret
Weapons Tracker
A live intelligence registry of tracked illegal weapons circulating in San Andreas — type, origin, last known holder, and associated cases. Cross-referenceable against active investigations and HVC profiles.
  • Links weapons to case files and suspect profiles automatically
  • Provides City Council with verifiable weapons intelligence data
  • Supports prosecution with chain-of-custody tracking
Top Secret
Gang Intelligence Module
Structured intelligence profiles on active criminal organisations operating in San Andreas — membership estimates, territory mapping, known associates, and threat levels. Maintained by the dedicated Gang Intelligence Unit.
  • Enables City Council to see the organised crime landscape at a glance
  • Feeds directly into HVC targeting and resource allocation decisions
  • Eliminates siloed gang intel that dies with individual detectives
Handler Only
Informant Registry
A classified registry of active CIB informants — handler assignments, gang affiliations, active tasking, and operational history. Identities are protected under a separate encryption layer accessible only to handlers and Command.
  • Protects informant lives through strict compartmentalisation
  • Enables Command to audit handler relationships without exposure
  • Prevents duplicate recruitment of the same informant across teams
Command Eyes Only
High-Value Criminal Targets
Dedicated target profiles for San Andreas's most wanted — full dossiers including known associates, active warrants, threat assessments, last known locations, and recommended engagement protocols.
  • Provides City Council with structured accountability on major criminal targets
  • Enables multi-division coordination against single high-value targets
  • Live warrant status prevents operational delays in the field
Financial
Budget Ledger
A classified internal ledger tracking departmental operational budgets, personal expense card allocations, and transaction logs by division. Provides Command full financial accountability without external exposure.
  • Gives City Council direct financial accountability within NEXUS
  • Replaces informal expense tracking susceptible to error or manipulation
  • Full audit trail for every budgetary line item
Secure Comms
NMAIL — Internal Mail System
A fully encrypted internal mail system operating entirely within the NEXUS secure environment. Detectives compose, send, and receive classified communications — including file attachments — without ever touching an external email provider.
  • Zero reliance on external email for classified operational communications
  • Full attachment support with image draft previews inside the secure portal
  • Notification bell and unread badge keep detectives informed without leaving NEXUS
  • Audit trail of all inter-division communications at Command's discretion
Documentation
Investigation Reports (Form 0001)
The standardised CIB Form 0001 investigation report module. Detectives file, review, and archive case reports entirely within NEXUS — structured documentation from first contact to case closure, accessible to Council-tier clearance at any time.
  • Standardised form eliminates incomplete or legally non-compliant filings
  • Full case archive accessible to Command and Council clearance tiers
  • PDF export capability for prosecution submissions and inter-agency handoffs
  • Every report timestamped and linked to the filing detective's authenticated session
Weekly Intelligence
Weekly Report — CIB Division
A structured weekly operational summary produced by CIB Command — covering case openings, closures, arrests, key intelligence developments, and divisional performance. Submitted in-portal and retained as an auditable operational record for the bureau and the City Council.
  • Provides City Council a reliable weekly accountability snapshot of CIB activity
  • Replaces informal verbal or off-portal reporting that leaves no audit trail
  • Searchable historical archive allows trend analysis across reporting periods
  • All personnel with appropriate clearance can review current and past reports
GIU · Top Secret
Weekly Report — GIU Division
A classified, separately gated weekly intelligence summary produced by the Gang Intelligence Unit. Access is restricted to GIU-credentialed detectives and above — the most sensitive operational intelligence is never mixed with general CIB access tiers.
  • GIU clearance gate ensures gang intelligence never reaches unauthorised tiers
  • Delivers structured organised-crime intelligence directly to City Council when requested
  • Compartmentalised from CIB weekly report — separate module, separate access control
  • Supports GIU accountability to both CIB Command and the State Council
Public Affairs
Public Announcements
A dedicated module for CIB's official public-facing communications — press releases, community safety notices, operation outcome statements, and inter-agency announcements. Drafted inside NEXUS by Command, then released through official public channels.
  • Centralises public communications drafting within the secure portal before release
  • Ensures Command approval and consistency before any statement reaches the public
  • Provides City Council visibility into CIB's public accountability posture
  • Archived within NEXUS for governance and historical record

Section III · Value Proposition
Why NEXUS Must Be Funded

Value to the City Council & CIB

The City Council does not merely fund an intelligence portal. It funds the operational capability, accountability infrastructure, and institutional memory of its primary investigative bureau. Without NEXUS, CIB reverts to fragmented, insecure, and unaccountable operations.

01
Operational Cohesion
Before NEXUS, intelligence lived in individual detective notes, unsecured chats, and disconnected documents. NEXUS unifies every data point — suspects, weapons, gang structures, active cases — into one authenticated session. The result: faster decisions, fewer coordination failures, and no intelligence lost when a detective leaves the bureau.
02
Council Accountability
NEXUS provides the City Council a direct window into CIB operations: financial ledgers, case counts, HVC target progress, and weekly divisional reports are all structured, auditable, and accessible to Council-tier clearance. Funding CIB without NEXUS means funding a black box. With NEXUS, every coin is traceable.
03
Security by Design
Every piece of intelligence in NEXUS exists behind clearance-gated authentication with full audit logging. Informant identities, HVC profiles, and gang intel never touch the open web. The alternative — detectives sharing this data via unsecured channels — represents a catastrophic operational security failure that NEXUS eliminates permanently.
04
Institutional Memory
Detectives rotate. Detectives resign. Criminal organisations do not. Without NEXUS, every personnel change bleeds intelligence that took months to build. The Weapons Tracker, Gang Intel module, and Informant Registry ensure that institutional knowledge survives turnover — the bureau remains effective regardless of who is in the field.
05
Prosecutorial Strength
Cases are won and lost on documentation. NEXUS's standardised CIB Form 0001, in-portal penal code reference, and structured evidence logs ensure that every investigation is documented to legal standard. Sloppy or incomplete reports — the kind that sink prosecutions — are systematically eliminated.
06
City-Wide Safety ROI
The Gang Intel and HVC modules give Command and the City Council a current, structured picture of the organised crime landscape in San Andreas. Resource allocation, joint operations, and prioritisation decisions are backed by data rather than intuition — directly improving the efficiency of every dollar the Council invests in law enforcement.

Section V · Security
Operational Security Architecture

Built to Be Impenetrable.

NEXUS's security model is not an afterthought. Every module, every data path, and every session is governed by a layered security architecture designed to make unauthorised access operationally impossible and every authorised action fully auditable.

[ AUTH ]
Role-Based Authentication
Five clearance tiers — Unclassified, Restricted, Confidential, Secret, and Top Secret — each with precisely scoped module access. No detective can access data above their clearance tier regardless of technical knowledge.
[ AUDIT ]
Full Audit Logging
Every session, every data access, and every modification is timestamped and logged. Command and Council can pull complete audit trails for any detective, any module, or any time window at any point.
[ COMMS ]
Encrypted Internal Communications
NMAIL operates entirely within NEXUS's secure environment. Classified operational communications never traverse external email infrastructure — zero interception surface, zero external exposure by architectural design.
[ IDFX ]
Informant Identity Protection
The Informant Registry operates behind a second compartmentalisation layer. Identities are accessible only to the assigned handler and Command-level clearance — structurally separate from all other modules.
[ GATE ]
Access Gate Verification
Every NEXUS session begins with a clearance verification sequence. Sessions expire after 30 minutes of inactivity. Shared credentials or session hijacking attempts are flagged to Command automatically.
[ ISOL ]
No External Data Paths
NEXUS does not connect to public APIs, external databases, or third-party services for any intelligence module. All sensitive data is contained within CIB-controlled infrastructure. The attack surface is zero by architecture.
