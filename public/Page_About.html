History of the Bureau
Feb 2026
Foundation & Deployment of CID
The Central Investigation Bureau was established by executive directive of the SAST Commissioner Andrew Gunner in response to a sharp rise in organized criminal activity across San Andreas. Originally a small task force of two officers, The CID then founded by Commander Diraj Lakota and soon led by Captain Elias Makya.
Expansion & Deployment of GRD
Following early operational successes, the Bureau was formally restructured into specialized divisions вЂ” the Gang Recon Division (GRD) led by Meifanny Lorenta and Criminal Investigation Division (CID) were established, giving the CIB a permanent institutional framework and dedicated personnel streams for long-term intelligence collection.
Mar 2026
Interagency Integration
The Bureau initiated meeting formal cooperation agreements with the San Andreas State Council, LSPD, LSCS, and the State Trooper, enabling real-time intelligence sharing and joint operational command. This milestone significantly extended the CIB's reach into both urban and rural criminal networks.
Directive One
Core Mission

The Central Intelligence Bureau exists to protect the people and institutions of San Andreas from organized criminal enterprise through intelligence-led law enforcement. Our mission is not reactive вЂ” it is anticipatory, strategic, and sustained.

We operate at the intersection of intelligence collection, criminal investigation, and field operations, ensuring that no criminal network can operate within San Andreas without the Bureau's awareness. Every resource, every operation, and every officer within the CIB is oriented toward a single purpose: the suppression and dismantlement of organized crime.

The CIB upholds the rule of law without compromise вЂ” employing lawful, rigorous, and ethical methods in every operation. We are accountable to the people of San Andreas and to the principles upon which the SAPD was founded.

Legal Authority
Jurisdiction

The Central Intelligence Bureau operates under the full legal authority of the San Andreas Police Department and exercises jurisdiction across the entire territory of the State of San Andreas. This authority is codified under the San Andreas Law Enforcement Intelligence Act, which grants the CIB broad powers to investigate, surveil, and prosecute organized criminal activity wherever it occurs within state borders.

The Bureau's jurisdiction is not limited to any single municipality or county. Unlike standard SAPD divisions вЂ” which operate within defined precinct boundaries вЂ” the CIB's mandate is statewide, enabling investigators to pursue criminal organizations that deliberately operate across jurisdictional lines to evade detection.

The CIB also holds authority to coordinate with and support federal law enforcement agencies in matters involving interstate criminal enterprises, and may exercise concurrent jurisdiction alongside federal bodies where applicable under existing bilateral agreements.

Arrest Authority вЂ” CIB officers hold full sworn peace officer status and may effect lawful arrests anywhere within the State of San Andreas.
Search & Surveillance вЂ” The Bureau may apply for and execute search warrants, wiretap orders, and surveillance authorizations under applicable state statute.
